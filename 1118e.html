Classic Candlestick Patterns 1-18: Complete Guide (Red-Up/Green-Down)

Page Summary

This page integrates 18 classic candlestick patterns, all adjusted to the "Red for Up, Green for Down" color scheme common in Taiwanese and Chinese markets.
The content is divided into two main parts:

  • Patterns 1-8: Classic Single-Stick Patterns
  • Patterns 9-18: Double and Triple-Stick Patterns
All charts are SVG vector graphics, ensuring they can be zoomed indefinitely without losing quality, ideal for teaching or printing.

8 Classic Single-Stick Candlestick Patterns (Patterns 1-8)

1. Bullish Marubozu
Marubozu (Bullish)
A strong bullish candle with almost no wicks. The open is the low, and the close is the high. Bulls were in complete control.
Strong Bullish Signal
2. Bearish Marubozu
Marubozu (Bearish)
A strong bearish candle with almost no wicks. The open is the high, and the close is the low. Bears were in complete control.
Strong Bearish Signal
3. Hammer
Hammer
Appears at the bottom of a downtrend. Lower wick is ≥ 2x the body. Very short or no upper wick. Shows bears failed and bulls strongly countered.
Bullish Reversal Signal
4. Hanging Man
Hanging Man
Appears at the top of an uptrend. Identical shape to a Hammer but with the opposite meaning. Shows a potential failure of the bulls.
Bearish Reversal Signal
5. Inverted Hammer
Inverted Hammer
Appears at the bottom of a downtrend. Long upper wick, very short lower wick. Shows bulls tried to rally but haven't fully succeeded.
Bullish Reversal (Needs Confirmation)
6. Shooting Star
Shooting Star
Appears at the top of an uptrend. Long upper wick, very short lower wick. Shows bulls rallied but were strongly rejected by bears.
Bearish Reversal Signal
7. Spinning Top
Spinning Top
Small body with long upper and lower wicks. Indicates intense indecision between bulls and bears. Signals a potential trend change or consolidation.
Indecision / Potential Reversal
8. Doji
Doji
Open price ≈ Close price. Represents perfect equilibrium between bulls and bears. Often a key reversal signal (depending on context).
Strong Reversal or Indecision Signal

Classic Candlestick Patterns (Patterns 9-18) - Double & Triple-Stick Formations

9. Bullish Engulfing
Bullish Engulfing
A large bullish candle's real body completely engulfs the prior bearish candle's body.
Bearish power is completely suppressed by bulls. Often at downtrend bottoms.
Strong Bottom Reversal Signal
10. Bearish Engulfing
Bearish Engulfing
A large bearish candle's real body completely engulfs the prior bullish candle's body.
Bullish power is completely suppressed by bears. Often at uptrend tops.
Strong Top Reversal Signal
11. Piercing Pattern
Piercing Pattern
A large bearish candle is followed by a bullish candle that closes above the 50% mark of the first candle's body.
Bulls launch a strong counter-attack, 'piercing' the bear's defense.
Bottom Bullish Reversal
12. Dark Cloud Cover
Dark Cloud Cover
A large bullish candle is followed by a bearish candle that closes below the 50% mark of the first candle's body.
Bears strike back, casting a 'dark cloud' over the bulls.
Top Bearish Reversal
13. Tweezer Bottom
Tweezer Bottom
Two candles have the same or nearly identical low prices, forming a horizontal support level.
Indicates strong buying support at this price.
Bottom Bullish Reversal
14. Tweezer Top
Tweezer Top
Two candles have the same or nearly identical high prices, forming a horizontal resistance level.
Indicates strong selling pressure at this price.
Top Bearish Reversal
15. Three White Soldiers
Three White Soldiers
Three consecutive medium-to-long bullish candles. Each opens within the previous body and closes at a new high.
Represents a steady bullish advance.
Strong Bullish Continuation
16. Three Black Crows
Three Black Crows
Three consecutive medium-to-long bearish candles. Each opens within the previous body and closes at a new low.
Represents a steady bearish press.
Strong Bearish Continuation
17. Morning Star
Morning Star
Large Bearish Candle → Small Body (gaps down) → Large Bullish Candle.
Bearish momentum is exhausted. Bulls take over. A classic bottom reversal.
Very Strong Bottom Bullish Reversal
18. Evening Star
Evening Star
Large Bullish Candle → Small Body (gaps up) → Large Bearish Candle.
Bullish momentum is exhausted. Bears take over. A classic top reversal.
Very Strong Top Bearish Reversal
